Thesis

In the late 1800s to early 1900s many aspects of consumer goods were unsubstantial and lead to injury and illness. Many corporations produced rotten and filthy food, and mercury riddled “cure-all” medications which claimed to cure anything from a headache to terminal illnesses. Fortunately, reporters and writers such as Upton Sinclair, Writer of “The Jungle,” brought light to the situation, which directly leading to the Pure Food and Drug Act of 1906. All corporations were forced to regulate all products to strict guidelines leading to greatly improved conditions for their products, protecting the right to unadulterated and safe food and medication.
